Approve Investments

The "Approve Investment" process is a critical component of the SAP project, designed to streamline and enhance the investment approval workflow within the organization. Process integrates seamlessly with other modules of the SAP system, such as financial management and project management, ensuring data consistency, and eliminating redundancies. Through this integration, the process enables real-time tracking of approved investments, facilitates effective budgeting and forecasting, and enhances overall financial governance within the organization.

In the "Approve Investments" process, the requester is responsible for completing decision points in the investment proposal, providing the necessary information related to the project. For larger investments exceeding a specific threshold (e.g. 100,000 Euro, an appropriation request (AR) is created, including detailed documentation justifying the investment. Once these steps are completed, the requester submits the investment proposal for approval.

The investment approver receives the proposal and evaluates it based on predetermined criteria such as financial viability, strategic alignment, and risk analysis. If the investment is approved, the investment manager sets the portfolio item status to "approved" and transfers it to the execution process, enabling further steps for project planning and implementation.

If the investment is rejected, the investment manager sets the portfolio item status to "canceled" and informs the project manager about the rejection, providing necessary feedback or reasons for the decision. In cases where a final decision cannot be reached immediately, the investment manager may request a review, changing the portfolio item status to "registered” for clarification and modification required on Appropriation Request.

During the review process, the project manager is informed about the need for further assessment or clarification. The portfolio item undergoes a thorough review to address any outstanding issues or concerns before a final decision is made.

The updated "Approve Investments" process ensures that investment proposals are handled appropriately based on their nature and value. It provides a clear path for decision-making, communicates outcomes effectively, and allows for necessary reviews when required. By following this process, organizations can make informed investment decisions while maintaining transparency and accountability throughout the SAP project.

AR

In SAP PPM, an Appropriation Request is a formal request for approval of the Project along with its project specification and financial data. The AR form is used to gather and document information about the request, helping the organizations make informed decisions regarding the Project. Key details captured in AR form include details of Project, organizational elements, Investment proposal, financial metrics, Budget information, Justifications, Business case. This design specification covers the form for Supplement AR needed during Execution Gate G4.

Below are the types of AR Form document defined for Bayer organization during the different Gate phases of Project life cycle:

  • B & C (Business and Concept) AR- Business planning Gate G1
  • Pre-fund AR- Concept definition Gate G2
  • Main AR- Basic Design Gate G3
  • Supplement AR- Execution Gate G4
  • Validation AR
