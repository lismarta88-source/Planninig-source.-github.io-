SAP Portfolio and Project Management is a tool that will help to monitor the projects and portfolio of projects and make decisions about realization and development of these Projects. SAP PPM enables to better control and innovate projects, processes, products, and services across their life cycles. It allows us to make decisions on clear and valuable information like portfolio value, balance, financial figures, and strategic alignment which provides decision support to understand current portfolios and projects in portfolios.

Business objects to describe business process:

Bucket

A bucket represents a structural element of a portfolio and reflects how portfolio items are organized within the overall portfolio hierarchy. Each portfolio item is assigned to exactly one bucket, and each bucket contains a defined set of portfolio items.

Buckets can belong either to the standard portfolio structure or to a classification hierarchy. Together, they form the backbone of the portfolio and reflect the company’s strategic organization.

Buckets enable financial and capacity planning at an aggregated level. Planning data at bucket level can be compared with individual portfolio items, and detailed information is available via the reporting cockpit.

Standard Portfolio Bucket Hierarchy

The standard portfolio structure consists of the following hierarchy levels:

  • Division
  • Function
  • Sub‑Function
  • Site + Legal Entity
  • Project

Portfolio Item

Generic object representing for example proposals, projects, concepts (anything that should be analyzed within a portfolio). Portfolio items can have dependencies to other items. You can create versions of a portfolio item. Each portfolio item consists of a set of attributes and can have services (e.g., questionnaires, scoring models) assigned to it. Portfolio item can be assigned at the lowest level buckets. It can be assigned to one or more buckets within the classification hierarchy.

Collection

A set of related portfolio items with a common strategic goal. Collections represent, for example, initiatives or programs within portfolio planning. A collection has its own reporting cockpit to enable portfolio managers to monitor the performance of the collection. A collection defines a subset of items and offers a way of structuring items that is different to the hierarchy of buckets. It can contain items from different buckets.

Classification Hierarchy

An arrangement of buckets within a portfolio structure that is an alternative to the standard portfolio hierarchy. Used to create a different bucket structure consisting of the items and initiatives from the standard portfolio hierarchy. Often used to represent the organizational setup of the company. In a classification hierarchy, existing business objects from the standard portfolio hierarchy are assigned and reviews, collections, and buckets can be performed.

2.4 Create and maintain bucket

To achieve a company's objectives during a specific time period, a coordinated group of portfolio items is managed. The strategic structure of a company is reflected in its portfolio, which may consist of one overall portfolio or separate portfolios managed independently. Each portfolio is a distinct entity and not interrelated with others. The portfolio's hierarchical structure is evident through the categorization of portfolio items into specific buckets. Financial and capacity planning can be conducted on these buckets and compared with the planning data of individual items. Information on portfolio items within a bucket can be obtained from a reporting cockpit.

The overall structure of the portfolio is reflected in the hierarchy of buckets. Each portfolio item falls into a certain bucket within the portfolio. One bucket consists of a set of portfolio items. Each bucket is part of the overall portfolio structure.

2.5 Portfolio Item Types

The following cases should reflect in Portfolio Items (and be represented as separate Portfolio Item types):

  • Lumpsum Project- Small Project fitting into the execution of the site with simplified approval process with (one step) AR Approval (Without Capital Planning G0, B&C, Pre-fund and Supplemental) with Capital planning FEL0 and Basic design FEL3 gates.

  • Lumpsum Budget - Lumpsum Budget Project are the placeholders which are used for to accommodate lumpsum budget for overall projects with simple approval process (one step) AR Approval (Without Capital Planning G0, B&C, Pre-fund and Supplemental) with Capital planning FEL0 gates.

  • Property, plant, and equipment (PPE), e.g.

  • Land, office buildings and other real estate transactions within the meaning of Corporate Directive No. 2084

  • Operating equipment and machinery

  • Devices and installations

  • Information technology (hardware)

  • Restructuring measures

  • Leases

  • Intangible Assets

  • Only Intangible Assets which can be capitalized. E.g., Information Technology (Software)
